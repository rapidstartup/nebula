## 5-Minute Elevator Pitch (No Slides Needed)

### The Hook (30 seconds)
"Most DAOs aren't actually democratic. They're plutocracies where the richest token holders control everything. We're fixing that."

### The Problem (60 seconds)
- Token-weighted voting = one dollar, one vote
- Younger generations completely disengaged from traditional democracy
- Paper processes are slow, expensive, exclusionary
- Current DAO tools only work for crypto insiders

### Our Solution (90 seconds)
"Nebula is mobile-first governance that works for everyone:

1. **One person, one vote** - Identity verified, not token-weighted
2. **Works on your phone** - 10 seconds to vote, no crypto knowledge needed  
3. **Geographic communities** - Your neighborhood, municipality, cooperative
4. **Swiss-grade security** - eID integration for official use

Imagine proposing a new bike lane from your phone, your neighbors voting in real-time, and the city seeing verifiable community support instantly."

### The Proof (60 seconds)
- Smart contracts deployed to Ethereum testnet
- Frontend built with React, TypeScript, mobile-optimized
- Swiss eID integration in development
- 3 municipalities interested in pilots

### The Ask (30 seconds)
"We're seeking pilot partners - HOAs, cooperatives, municipalities. Want to see a demo?"

- Screen recording of localhost dev version
- Clear voiceover explaining each step
- Share via Loom link

### Script

**[0:00-0:30] Introduction**
"Hey, Nathan here with a quick demo of Nebula - democratic governance that actually works. Since our live site is getting some upgrades, I'm showing you the development version. Everything you see here is already built and working."

**[0:30-1:30] Creating a DAO**
"First, let's create a DAO. I'll call this 'Riverdale Neighborhood Association'. I can set it as a geographic DAO, which means it's tied to a real location. Set some basic voting rules - simple majority, 48-hour voting periods. And... created."

**[1:30-2:30] Identity Verification**
"Before people can vote, they verify their identity. This isn't KYC - it's proof of personhood. We use zero-knowledge proofs, so we know they're a unique human without knowing who they are. Privacy-preserving but accountable."

**[2:30-3:30] Submitting a Proposal**
"Now let's create a proposal. Say we want to approve a budget for a new community garden. I add a title, description, attach a budget spreadsheet. Set the voting options - yes, no, abstain. Submit."

**[3:30-4:30] Voting Experience**
"Here's what voting looks like on mobile. Open the app, see the proposal, tap your choice, confirm with biometrics. Done. Five seconds. Results update in real-time."

**[4:30-5:00] Results & Transparency**
"After voting closes, anyone can verify the results. It's recorded on the blockchain - transparent, tamper-proof, auditable. But personal votes remain private. Best of both worlds."

**[5:00-5:30] Close**
"That's Nebula. Democratic governance that just works. Want to pilot this with your community? Reach out: nathan@nebula.io. Thanks for watching."

## FAQ for Offline Conversations

### Technical Questions

**Q: Is it on mainnet or testnet?**
A: Currently on Sepolia testnet. Mainnet launch scheduled after security audit (funded by grant we're applying for).

**Q: What blockchain does it use?**
A: Ethereum for now. Multi-chain support planned - Polygon, Arbitrum, Optimism.

**Q: How does identity verification work?**
A: Multiple options: document verification with ZK proofs, social graph analysis, biometric options. Users choose their level. Swiss eID integration for official use.

**Q: Is it open source?**
A: Yes, MIT license. github.com/rapidstartup/nebula

### Business Questions

**Q: What's the pricing?**
A: Free for under 50 members. $0.50/member/month for 50-500. Custom for larger.

**Q: How long to set up?**
A: 5 minutes for basic DAO. 30 minutes with full customization.

**Q: Can we export our data?**
A: Absolutely. No vendor lock-in. All data exportable anytime.

**Q: What about security?**
A: Professional audit planned (part of grant). Bug bounty program. Gradual rollout.

### Pilot Questions

**Q: What's required for a pilot?**
A: 3-6 month commitment. 50-500 participants. Feedback sessions. Case study participation (optional).

**Q: Is there cost for pilot?**
A: No. Completely free during pilot period.

**Q: What support do you provide?**
A: Setup assistance, training, technical support throughout pilot.
